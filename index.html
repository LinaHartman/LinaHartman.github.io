HATE
Welcome to Eäros, mortal soul.
Step closer, let me see you. You're one of those, aren't you? No matter. We willl find out soon enough.
Wondering where you are? This, mortal, is the plane of Earos, created and ruled over by a triumvirate of deities. I am the God of Hate, you shall meet the others once I'm finished... with you. No need to tremble, mortal, you would be of no use to me any more dead than you already are. Besides, there are many forms and manifestations of hate, not all of them violent. You will grow to understand that yourself, as all mortal things do.
Anyhow, where was I... Ah yes, the creation of the world. In the beginning, it was just the three of us: God of Hate, Goddess of lust and the God of Greed. A time before ours, we do not remember and none we met did, whether there was anything before our time, we may never know, if ever there was a time without us...
We created the lands and the skies and the waters first, playgrounds to be populated later by our various and numerous children. Our first experiments, the Titans and the Elementals, we hadn't yet infused with our essence. In a way, they are little more than extensions of nature, so don't expect stimulating conversations with them. But their hunger for destruction, delicous. Anyhow, after them came the beasts of thousands upon thousands of shapes - unlike their predecessors, they have needs and relations, but no sense of self, no morality - just a drive for self-preservation. Watching them gets boring so fast... and their killing, out of pure instinct, it just wasn't good enough, not brutal enough, not enough rage!
Finally we separated the Celestial Sphere from the Mundane, and divided it in half to the Heavenly and the Infernal Circles. This allowed us to experiment with morality in an isolated environment, and to create avatars of pure spirit energy, so that through them we would be able to walk amongst mortals if we so wished. These avatars became the Angels and the Demons, known collectively and among deniers of morality as Greater Celestials.
All other sentient races have within their mortal bodies a fragment of our mixed divine essence, most often referred to as the soul. Our first masterpiece was the noble race of the Dragons. The soul of the dragons came in equal parts from the three of us, but we soon realised that the dynamics of a society are much more interesting if balance is reached within groups rather than within individuals. You see Dragons were too perfect, too much balance, so they didn't need help from each other. To this day, they are utterly individualistic creatures, but having been dethroned by our newer creations, they took to harbouring hatred and disdain in their hearts - and I won't turn them away now, will I?
These days, the three of us started our own little experiments, which lead to races after our own desires. Mine were the Ogres – juggernauts of pure hatred. While I do still enjoy them to this day, I came to the realisation that pure rage, without intent, is not brutal enough. Their drive to violence rendert them emotionless. And then I made the orcs. The very pinical of evolution to this day!
Now, I would very much like to make you an orc, but alas, that is not my duty- and weak human souls are not exactly compatible with orcish physiognomy. However, I've arranged the possibility for you to be born as a half-blood of sorts... Just something to keep in mind for when you come to that bridge, will you?
But enough talk! I've been blabbering on and on, why didn't you say something?! Time to make some choices. Look at the boxes bellow you. They contain points, gold and most little something extra. You may open them all, but only keep the content of a single box. Any questions? No? Good then get to it!
Finally! Common, hurry up, we don't got all Eäos long! You should know that the attributes of your shell are almost wholly responsible for your strengths and weaknesses. Most mortal don't get a say in the pathetic shell they have to inhibit anyway. I will first offer you two choices. You can either allocate attributes to your soul randomly, the same way it happens to all other mortals of Earos and taht pitty plane you died in... Earth, is it? I will let you reroll for a prics. The other oh so boring option, seeing as your case is a little different, you can choose to balance your strengths and weaknesses as you desire. This latter option will leave you with slightly lower strengths, naturally, but you can worry about that later. So, which will it be, mortal?
The first, and, if you ask me, principal among them is strength. If you're expecting to be involved in physical altercations, I strongly recommend spending some points here. It also affects how much you can carry.
Well, common mortal. It's either Luck or Planning. Take your pick!

Yes, yes, I know that look. Pretence of disgust. Perhaps genuine, at least as far as you're concerned. Tell me, though, which of the three of us is present uniquely in creatures with not just a soul, but a sense of morality? Who among us is the pragmatic one? Whose influence is responsible for societal progress; what is the driving force behind every noble or ignoble intellectual pursuit? I see you're not convinced. Fair enough, let's take it a step further. We are in agreement at least in the fact that only races of heightened cognitive ability exhibit greedlike or greed-adjacent behaviours, correct? And you would argue that the relationship between them is correlative rather than causative, is that so? Surely, you wouldn't think they're coincidental - unless, of course, there's been a terrible mix-up, and you're just a scrawny ogre with baffling scholarly inclinations? No? Well, good. Better not act like it then, hm? Allow me to let you in on a little secret. I was not at all interested in the creation of Earos. I don't share my siblings' absurd interest in cosmic gardening or farming livestock. The reason they refer to me as their younger brother' has nothing to do with age - just didn't care to partake in their little project, until it got interesting. Sure, I'd created the goblins as a jest, a rather on the nose satire of my brother's unhinged orcs and my sister's sex-crazed succubi (although, as found out some time later, neither of them are completely without perspective); it is not my fault they are about as subtle as their creations, and so they took the joke seriously. Likewise, I was not terribly keen on engaging in the infinitely vain practice of creating my own races (goblins aside), especially since I'd already discovered your world. Well, the world that your soul came from, anyway. What? No, I wouldn't say stolen, but then again, I'm the only one with any semblance of tact or subtlety in this family. Or, indeed, critical thinking. Yes, I coordinated the transmigration of your soul to this plane, but 'stolen'? Everything can be stolen but the soul, and that is by definition.
